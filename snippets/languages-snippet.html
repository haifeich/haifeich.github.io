Languages Learning

Learning languages is always my passion. I even could say that I spend my life for learning languages. From China to Spain, and now in Swizerland, I can speak three foreign languages fluently, English, Spanish and French. In this learning process, I become more confident and it broadens my horizons. Here I will show you some of my favourite channels. Let's learn together!

French

I began to learn French since I arrived in Switzerland four years ago. I began with the prounciation, which is quite difficult for a Chinese native speaker. Do you want a try?

If you are facinate with grammar, I recommande you Learn French with Elsa. She can solve your troubles with well explained examples.

If you want to learn more expressions from native speaker, it's good to try with Français Authentique. They are a happy family, always talk about nice topics.

German

Learning German is in my future project. It's interesting that I feel lost each time when I'm in Swiss German speaking part. I'm very courios about it. Do you want to join me?

If you are totally beginner like me, I recommande you Nicos Weg. There are three interesting "movies" from A1 to B1 on Youtube, we can learn meanwhile feel their lovely life in Germany.

If you want to learn more expressions from native speaker, it's good to try with Easy German. They interview people in the streets in German with all kinds of interesting questions. You will be surprised by them!

English

Although I've learned English since I was a child at school. There's always many aspects that I can make some progress. As it's a worldwide language, each progress will be useful.

If you are struggled with the pronunciation, I recommande you Rachel's English. From her you can learn the difference from each letter, and what the American do when they speak fast. There are plenty of details to notice!

If you are interested in languages learning skills, I recommande you Robin MacPherson. He can speak eight languages fluently. In his Youtube channel he shares all his methods. There must be one suitable for you.

Spanish

I've lived in Spain for four years in three different cities. I learned and practiced Spanish, knew interesting people from all over the world. It's always nice to recall those moments.

If you don't speak Spanish quite well, I recommande you Español con Juan. He can always teach you a lot in a made-up story in a very dramatic way. It will be fun.

If you are interested in grammar, I recommande you Spanish with Vicente. He's always paciente, explains with examples in our daily life. I often listen to him when I'm doing excercises.
